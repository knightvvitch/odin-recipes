Chili

Got a chili contest coming up? Make this, and win at least a participation trophy.

Nothing like a bowl of steaming chili on a chilly Autumn day, am I right? Warm up those hands, heart, and belly with beans, ground meat, and veggies all spiced to perfection.

I want to preface this recipe with the fact that I am going to call for ground turkey because I only eat poultry and seafood, but you're more than welcome to use a ground meat of your choosing...venison, beef, soy, mutton,...I think lobster might be pretty weird, but whatever floats your boat.

Ingredients

  • 1 32 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cans of beans (kidney, pinto, etc.)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup broth (chicken, beef, veg, etc.) or beer
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 package of ground meat (or substitute)
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 2 tbsp cumin
  • 3 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Chop the pepper and onion and mince the garlic
  2. In a large pot over medium heat, saute the pepper, onion, and garlic in the oil for a few minutes
  3. Add the ground meat and seasonings and saute until mostly cooked
  4. Add the broth and let simmer for a few minutes
  5. Drain the beans and add them to the pot and simmer for a few minutes
  6. Add the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste and stir well
  7. Simmer on low for 15-20 minutes and sample - add any additional seasonings to your taste
  8. Cover and simmer on low for another 30 minutes or so
  9. Serve by itself or with cheese (cotija, queso fresco, or cheddar), sour cream, scallions, cilantro, lime, etc.
  10. Enjoy!
